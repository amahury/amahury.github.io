### About the project
This ERC-funded project aims to help develop more inclusive, theoretically sound, and socially just botanical research by focusing on virtual herbaria—digitized collections of plant specimens. From a perspective of integrated history and philosophy of science, it addresses several historical, epistemic, and conceptual challenges of herbarium research in light of current digitization trends. BOTLEG will collaborate with botanists (m/f/x) and herbarium experts (m/f/x) in Central America, Southeast Asia, and the Netherlands. More information on the project: https://tinyurl.com/yc236p2j

### Your Tasks
The successful candidate (m/f/x) is expected to actively contribute to the above project. The Post-Doctoral project should study the work and impact of (networks of) local and Indigenous plant collectors (19-20th century) in Central America (or Latin America more generally) and/or South East Asia. This work should be supported by digital humanities approaches, exploring biodiversity databases as historical sources, and/or archival research. A focus of the project should be on (or related to) issues of colonial history of botany, digitization and biases in natural history collections, epistemic injustice and traditional ecological knowledge.

The successful candidate (m/f/x) will be encouraged to present their research at international workshops or conferences, and at meetings of the international research group. Research collaborations with other team members (from Mexico, Indonesia, the Netherlands, Germany, and the U.S.) as well as with members of the Chair of History and Philosophy of Science are strongly encouraged. The Post-Doctoral researcher (m/f/x) will co-organize workshops and conferences with the other members of the team. The successful candidate (m/f/x) will enjoy the groups’ administrative facilities and financial support. In addition, there is the possibility, though no obligation, to conduct some teaching in English (or German).

### Your profile
A PhD in history, digital humanities, cultural studies, philosophy, or another relevant discipline. The candidate (m/f/x) should have expertise or some knowledge in one (or both) of the following fields:
1. History of Science or History of Colonialism
2. Digital Humanities (e.g., Digital Natural and Cultural Heritage)

They should have the (1) ability to substantively engage with historical sources (with reading skills in Dutch, Spanish or Bahasa Indonesia, or willingness to acquire these skills) and have experience in archival research and/or (2) experience in computational methods of text mining, analysis, and visualization (e.g. Python) or analysis of biodiversity data (e.g., R).

The candidates (m/f/x) should have a strong interest in integrating historical and philosophical studies of science in their work. Although no prior knowledge of history of biology is required, such knowledge will be considered as a plus. Other general requirements are high research, writing, and communication skills, the ability to work both independently and as part of an (international) team, and excellent command of English (mastering the German Language is not required).

The Ruhr University Bochum (RUB) is located in the dynamic metropolitan and multicultural region of the Ruhrgebiet (state of North-Rhine Westfalia, close to the Netherlands and Belgium). With its 20 faculties RUB houses 43,000 students from over 130 different nationalities, over 800 international researchers and visiting staff, and a broad range of disciplines on one compact campus, including the humanities and social sciences, science and technology, and health sciences. It offers rich opportunities for collaboration across different disciplines and one of the largest botanical gardens among German universities. The ‘Institutes of Philosophy’ (I and II) are the largest Philosophy Department in Germany, which provides an ideal environment for Post-Doctoral researchers to both broaden and deepen their expertise and personal skills to further their career. For more information on the Chair of History and Philosophy of Science, please visit: https://www.pe.rub.de/philosophie/i/wtwg/index.html.de
